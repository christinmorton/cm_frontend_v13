How Much Does a Custom WordPress Website Cost in 2026?

Let's cut to the chase: a custom WordPress website for a small business typically costs between $1,500 and $15,000. I know that's a wide range, but it depends on what you're building.

If you've been searching for pricing and found nothing but "contact us for a quote," you're not alone. Most developers are vague because every project is different. But you deserve real numbers before you start reaching out. Here's what actually drives the cost.

What Drives the Cost

The price of a website depends on a few key factors:

  • Complexity: A 5-page brochure site is a different project than a booking system or membership portal
  • Design: Template customization vs. fully custom design from scratch
  • Functionality: A contact form is simpler than e-commerce with inventory management
  • Content: Do you provide the copy and images, or do you need help creating them?
  • Ongoing needs: One-time build vs. maintenance relationship

A local restaurant needing a menu, hours, and reservation link is a very different project than a consultant needing a course platform with payments and member areas.

The Real Cost of "Cheap" Websites

You can find websites for $500 on Fiverr. You can build one yourself on Squarespace for $16/month. These options exist, and for some situations, they work fine.

But there are tradeoffs you should understand:

  • What you don't own: With most page builders, you're renting. If you leave, you start over.
  • What breaks when you want to change something: Cheap builds often use shortcuts that make future changes expensive.
  • Hidden costs: Plugins, themes, monthly fees, and migration costs add up over time.
  • The cost of a site that doesn't convert: A website that doesn't bring you customers is expensive at any price.

Key insight: Cheap now often means expensive later. A $500 website that you need to rebuild in two years costs more than a $3,500 website that lasts.

What You're Actually Paying For

When you hire a professional developer, you're not just paying for someone to make pages appear on the internet. You're paying for:

  • Discovery and strategy: Understanding your business and goals, not just building pages
  • Mobile optimization: A site that works perfectly on phones and tablets
  • Speed optimization: Fast loading times that keep visitors engaged
  • Security foundations: Proper setup to protect your site and visitors
  • SEO-ready structure: Built so Google can find and rank you
  • Training: So you can update content yourself without calling for help
  • Ownership: You get the keys to everything when we're done

Questions to Ask Before You Hire

Whether you hire me or someone else, these questions will help you evaluate any developer:

  • Do they explain what you'll own when the project ends?
  • What's included in the quote vs. what costs extra?
  • Who handles hosting, domain, and updates after launch?
  • Can you see examples of similar projects they've built?
  • What happens if you need changes after launch?

The Bottom Line

A website is an investment in a platform you control. The right investment depends on your business goals, your budget, and how important your online presence is to generating revenue.

Don't just compare prices compare what you're getting. A custom website that you own outright, that converts visitors to customers, and that can grow with your business is worth more than a cheap template that you'll need to replace.
