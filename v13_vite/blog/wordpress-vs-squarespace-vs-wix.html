WordPress vs Squarespace vs Wix: Which Should You Actually Choose?

There are hundreds of comparison articles out there, most of them affiliate-driven or outdated. Here's the real answer: it depends on what you need, and there's no universally "best" option.

But there's one thing most comparisons skip: ownership and long-term flexibility. That's what we'll focus on here.

The Quick Answer

Wix

Best for simple sites you want to build yourself with minimal learning curve. Limited flexibility long-term.

Squarespace

Best for visually-driven businesses (photographers, restaurants, portfolios) who want polished templates without code.

WordPress

Best for businesses that want full control, plan to grow, or need custom functionality.

What You Actually Own

This is the question most comparisons skip, and it's the most important one.

Wix and Squarespace: You're renting. Your site lives on their servers, uses their system. If you leave, you start over. If they change pricing or features, you adapt or migrate. You cannot take your site with you.

WordPress: You own the code and content. You can move hosts, hire any developer, modify anything. It's yours. If you stop paying your hosting bill, you can download everything and move it somewhere else.

The tradeoff: Ownership requires more responsibility. But for a business asset, that tradeoff usually makes sense.

Cost Comparison (Honest Version)

Don't just look at sticker prices. Look at what you'll actually pay over time.

Platform Monthly Cost Hidden Costs 3-Year Reality
Wix $17–$35/mo Apps, storage upgrades, domain $600–$1,500
Squarespace $16–$49/mo E-commerce fees, limited integrations $600–$1,800
WordPress $5–$50/mo hosting Theme, plugins, developer if needed $200–$2,000+

WordPress can be cheaper or more expensive depending on how you build it. Custom development is an investment, but you're building equity, not renting.

Flexibility and Growth

What happens when your business changes?

  • Wix: Adding complex features often means workarounds or switching platforms entirely.
  • Squarespace: Great within its constraints, but those constraints are real. E-commerce is limited compared to dedicated solutions.
  • WordPress: Nearly unlimited flexibility. Booking systems, membership areas, custom integrations, headless architecture. It can grow with you.

Example: A service business that starts with a simple site but later wants client portals and automated invoicing will find WordPress adapts. With Squarespace, they'd likely need to migrate.

Ease of Use (The Real Story)

The question isn't "which is easiest" but "what kind of easy do you need?"

  • Wix: Easiest to start. Drag and drop. But can become messy as you add more.
  • Squarespace: Clean interface, but more rigid. You work within their structure.
  • WordPress: Steeper learning curve, but most business owners can handle basic updates after training. Complex changes need a developer.

SEO and Performance

Let's not oversell this:

  • All three can rank in Google if done well
  • WordPress offers more control over technical SEO
  • Squarespace and Wix have improved but still have limitations
  • Page speed varies by how you build, not just platform

For a simple site, the SEO difference is minimal. For complex sites or competitive keywords, WordPress gives you more tools.

So Which Should You Choose?

Choose Wix if:

  • You need something up fast and simple
  • You're comfortable with limited customization
  • Budget is tight and you're doing it yourself

Choose Squarespace if:

  • Visual design matters most (creative businesses)
  • You want polished templates without touching code
  • Your needs are unlikely to change dramatically

Choose WordPress if:

  • You're building a long-term business asset
  • You want to own your platform
  • You anticipate needing custom features or integrations
  • You're willing to invest in professional development

The Bottom Line

The core question is: what kind of business are you building?

A website you rent works for some situations. A platform you own makes sense for others. There's no wrong answer just the right answer for your specific needs.
